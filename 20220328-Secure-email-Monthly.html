Some tips to make your account secure
At TradingView, we’re constantly working to ensure your account is secure and well-protected. However, the most important steps to security have to be taken by the user. Here are a few tips you can do right now to make your TradingView account as secure as possible:
Set up two-factor authentication if you are
still not using it
This is a highly effective way to protect your account because even if your password is stolen, it won’t be enough for an intruder to gain access. This is the single best way to secure your account and we recommend that you enable 2‑factor authentication in your profile settings right now. Check out the details here.
Do not use one password for all sites and applications
If you do this, a breach of security on one site could mean a breach for all of the sites and applications you use. It gives hackers access to various accounts of yours, and this is easily avoidable.
Use strong passwords
Your password should be difficult to guess, for both potential hackers and other computers. We recommend using a randomly generated password with a length of 12 characters and more. If you use a password manager, this can be very simple. When thinking of a new password, please remember that length is more important than special characters (i.e. @,#,!,/,<,?,%).
Check for breaches
Check if your account has been breached and/or leaked by any commonly used websites or services. You can use either Predictive Phishing Protection in Google Chrome or try Firefox Monitor or haveibeenpwned.com. Change your password immediately as soon as you know your account/email and/or password have been breached.
Use our app
Use our official TradingView app to avoid any breaches of data. Our mobile app is available on Google Play for Android and App Store for iOS.
Avoid using third-party services
Never enter your TradingView account login credentials for authentication on any third‑ party website services and/or apps.
Opt for reliability
Use reliable anti-malware solutions (also known as anti‑virus software) in order to protect your data.
Thank you
We hope this guide helps you secure your account and we hope you take each piece of advice seriously. Your security is important to us.
